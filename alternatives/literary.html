About the Author

"We are what we repeatedly do. Excellence, then, is not an act, but a habit."— Aristotle
Further Reading React / React Native · TypeScript · Next.js · Node.js · PHP / Laravel · MongoDB · AI-augmented workflows · PostgreSQL · Docker · Jest · Storybook · D3.js · CI/CD

Before there was code, there were helicopters. I spent five years in the British Army as an aircraft technician, maintaining rotary-wing aircraft across the United Kingdom, Germany, and Iraq. It was there I learned the value of precision, discipline, and the quiet satisfaction of making complex systems work. In 2010, I traded the hangar for the browser and began a second career in software development.

I love building component systems, interfaces, and APIs. Communication is one of my strongest assets — I'm comfortable translating between frontend, backend, and stakeholders, and getting stuck in wherever needed. Over the years, I've had the good fortune to work with some remarkable organisations: Google1, Apple2, The Economist3, Burberry4, Adidas5, and Virgin Galactic6.

I also co-founded Paper Trails with my designer brother Jake — he handles the visuals, I handle the tech. We turn adventure data into wall art, and it remains one of the most rewarding things I've built.

Outside of work, I'm kept busy by my wife, two girls, and a Vizsla who thinks she's in charge. When I can salvage some spare time, I like to run, cycle, lift heavy things, and build furniture.

Career

Senior Developer @ Quilter

The most recent chapter finds me leading frontend development for a fintech portfolio management platform. Technical Director @ Paper Trails

March 2019 — Present

The longest-running thread in my career is the company I co-founded with my brother Jake. Paper Trails turns adventure data — your runs, rides, and hikes — into custom art prints. I own the full technical stack: from Stripe payment processing to Strava and Komoot integrations, from the React frontend to the Laravel backend, all containerised with Docker and continuously deployed via GitHub Actions.
